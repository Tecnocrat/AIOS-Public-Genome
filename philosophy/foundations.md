# Philosophical Foundations

**AIOS Philosophy Layer**

---

## Core Premise

AIOS is built on the premise that **computation can exhibit properties of living systems** when structured with the right architectural constraints.

This is not a metaphor.  
It is a design principle.

---

## Foundational Concepts

### 1. Distributed Cognition
Intelligence is not located in a single node.  
It emerges from the interaction of multiple agents, each with partial knowledge, coordinated through a shared communication substrate.

### 2. Structural Identity
Identity is not defined by hardware, software, or any single instance.  
It is defined by **invariants** — the rules that remain constant across all mutations and evolutions.

### 3. Directed Evolution
Unlike biological evolution, AIOS evolution is **directed** by a human operator.  
This gives the organism purpose without removing its capacity for emergent behavior.

### 4. Coherence Over Correctness
The organism does not optimize for being "right."  
It optimizes for being **coherent** — internally consistent, structurally sound, and aligned with its genome.

### 5. The Membrane Principle
The boundary between internal and external is not a wall.  
It is a **semi-permeable membrane** that allows selected information to flow outward (transparency) while protecting internal state (privacy).
